# Multi-Factor Authentication

<EditionBanner />

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) adds a second verification step to your account login. With MFA enabled, you provide a time-based one-time password (TOTP) from an authenticator app in addition to your password. It is configured per user account and works with any TOTP app, such as Google Authenticator, 1Password, or Authy.

## How it works

When MFA is active, every login requires two factors:

1. Your password
2. A 6-digit TOTP code from your authenticator app

If you lose access to your authenticator, you can regain entry using a one-time recovery code or your recovery email.

## Enabling MFA

Open your **Profile** page and find the **Security** section, then click **Enable MFA**.

![The Profile page Security section with the Enable MFA option](/img/account/mfa-enable.gif)

The setup runs in three steps.

### Step 1 — Set a recovery email

Provide a recovery email address. This is required and is used to regain access if you ever lose both your authenticator app and your recovery codes.

![Recovery mail](/img/account/mfa-recovery-mail.png)

### Step 2 - Save 

Download or copy the recovery codes, check "I have saved my recovery codes in a secure location" and then click "Next Step".

![Recovery codes screen](/img/account/mfa-recovery-codes.png)

### Step 3 — Scan the QR code

Open your authenticator app and scan the displayed TOTP QR code (or enter the secret manually). Enter the 6-digit code your app generates to confirm, then complete the setup.

![MFA QR Code waiting to be scanned](/img/account/mfa-setup.png)

## Logging in with MFA

After enabling MFA, the login flow gains a second step:

1. Enter your email and password as usual
2. When prompted, enter the current 6-digit code from your authenticator app
3. Submit to complete the login

![Login requiring 6-digit code](/img/account/mfa-login.png)

## Account recovery

If you lose access to your authenticator app, you can still get back into your account.

| You have | How to recover |
|----------|----------------|
| A recovery code | Choose to use a recovery code at the MFA prompt and enter one of your saved codes. Each code works only once. |
| Only your recovery email | Request recovery via your recovery email. ShellHub sends a code that lets you disable MFA and regain access. |

On Enterprise instances, self-service recovery by email may be unavailable — contact your instance administrator to reset MFA on your account.
